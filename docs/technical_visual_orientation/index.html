1. HSR-specific transport mission variable

In this repository, the mission variable is not commercial extraction yield, not riser certification, and not seabed mining readiness. It is sustained slurry-transport viability: the reduced surrogate maintains upward transport of REY mud-like suspended solids without crossing into deposition-limited loss, blackout/coastdown collapse, or restart-fragile regimes under pump/flow-margin constraints.

2. Reduced lifting / transport surrogate

A10-HSR should be read as a reduced transport-transition theory for ultra-deep REY mud lifting. It does not define a certified riser, pump train, ship system, subsea mining operation, environmental impact statement, or commercial extraction workflow. The useful object is the diagnostic map of flow margin, suspended solids, deposition thresholds, blackout/coastdown response, and restart viability.

HSR-specific surrogate reading

  • The state channels are transport diagnostics: flow velocity, slurry/solids load, critical velocity, pump margin, deposition risk, and restart margin.
  • The model is reduced and pre-engineering; it is not an offshore design package.
  • The value is in transition classification and failure-mode separation.
  • Environmental approval, offshore certification, and commercial extraction feasibility are outside scope.

4. Regime split: sustained transport versus collapse

A project-specific strength of this archive is the distinction between transport regimes: sustained lift, near-threshold transport, deposition-limited loss, blackout/coastdown collapse, and restart-fragile states. These are diagnostic categories, not offshore deployment readiness levels.

Diagnostic interpretation

  • Sustained transport in a reduced surrogate is not a certified riser design.
  • Near-threshold transport is not a safe operational margin.
  • Blackout/coastdown survival is not a restart procedure.
  • Environmental and industrial validation remain separate requirements.
