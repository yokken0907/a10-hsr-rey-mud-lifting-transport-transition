# Claim Boundary

## Allowed claims

- This repository accompanies an AI-assisted independent research manuscript on HSR v3.3.
- HSR v3.3 is a reduced-order pre-engineering transport-transition theory for ultra-deep REY mud lifting.
- The manuscript distinguishes sustained-transport, fault-induced collapse, and deposition-limited regimes.
- In the current reduced-order model, the no-blackout critical inlet velocity is approximately uss ≈ 1.25–1.26.
- At uss = 1.6, no-blackout sustained transport persists over 40 s, 80 s, and 120 s, while deposition creep becomes the next constraint.
- Residual pressure-gradient time derivative is a candidate diagnostic trigger in the tested reduced model.

## Forbidden claims

- This is a field-validated deep-sea mining system.
- This is final riser hardware design.
- This has sea-trial validation.
- This is production-ready offshore mining equipment.
- This provides environmental permitting readiness.
- This provides industrial safety certification.
- This solves long-duration deposition management completely.
- This replaces full 3D CFD/FSI, field testing, or offshore engineering review.

## Standard short wording

> HSR v3.3 is a reduced-order pre-engineering transport-transition theory for ultra-deep REY mud lifting. It is not a sea-trial-validated mining system, final riser design, or certified offshore deployment method.
